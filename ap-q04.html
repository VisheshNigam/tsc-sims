Q4 Energy — Conservation on a Ramp
A 2 kg block is released from rest at the top of a frictionless ramp of height 1.8 m. What is its speed at the bottom?  (g = 10 m/s²)
✓ Correct Answer
B — v = √(2gh) = √36 = 6 m/s
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "forgot the square root" trap (option D): students compute 2gh = 2·10·1.8 = 36 and stop. But that's v², not v. You still have to take the square root → v = 6 m/s.

The "mass matters" trap: notice that mass cancels out. mgh = ½mv² → v = √(2gh). The block could be 2 kg or 200 kg — same speed at the bottom. Energy conservation is mass-independent for a free slide.

The "ramp shape matters" trap: the answer doesn't depend on the angle or shape of the ramp — only on the vertical drop h. A long shallow ramp and a short steep one give the same final speed (with no friction).
