Q5 Electrostatics L2 · Numerical
Charges +4 μC and −4 μC are placed 0.2 m apart. The magnitude of the electric field at the midpoint between them is:  (k = 9 × 10⁹ N m² C⁻²)
✓ Correct Answer
D — 1.44 × 10⁷ N/C
💡 Don't Fall For This
The cancellation trap (→ 0 N/C): Many students assume opposite charges cancel the field at the midpoint — they don't. The fields from +q and −q both point in the same direction there, so they add, not cancel.

Forgetting to double (→ 3.6 × 10⁶): Each charge contributes E = kq/r² = 3.6 × 10⁶ N/C, but there are two charges both contributing in the same direction. Etotal = 2E = 1.44 × 10⁷ N/C.

Using full separation as r (→ 7.2 × 10⁶): r is the distance from each charge to the midpoint — that's 0.1 m, not 0.2 m. Using r = 0.2 m gives a value 4× too small.
