Q9 Rotation — Rolling Without Slipping
A solid disk and a hoop, both of mass M and radius R, are released from rest at the top of two identical frictionless inclines. Both roll without slipping. Which reaches the bottom first?
✓ Correct Answer
D — The disk wins. v_disk = √(4gh/3) > v_hoop = √(gh)
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "they tie" trap (option A): for objects sliding without rotation, mgh = ½mv² and mass cancels — same speed for everyone. But here both objects are rolling, so the energy splits between translation (½mv²) and rotation (½Iω²). How much goes to rotation depends on I, and that's where they differ.

The "more inertia helps" traps (B and C): more rotational inertia means more energy is locked up in rotation — that's a penalty, not a bonus. The hoop has I = MR² (all its mass at the rim), the disk has I = ½MR² (mass distributed inside). The hoop pays a bigger rotational tax, leaving less energy for translation.

The clean math: with energy conservation, v² = 2gh / (1 + I/(MR²)). For the disk, I/MR² = ½ → v² = (4/3)gh. For the hoop, I/MR² = 1 → v² = gh. v_disk / v_hoop = √(4/3) ≈ 1.15. The disk arrives first by a clear margin.
