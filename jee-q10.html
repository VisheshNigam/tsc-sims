Q10 Waves & Superposition L3 · Numerical
Two waves y₁ = A sin(ωt) and y₂ = A sin(ωt + 2π/3) are superimposed. The resultant amplitude is:
✓ Correct Answer
B — A
💡 Don't Fall For This
Picking Zero (→ full cancellation): Δφ = π gives zero, but Δφ = 2π/3 is only 120° — not 180°. Students who memorise "phase difference → zero" without applying the formula get caught here.

Picking 2A (→ constructive): Full constructive interference only happens at Δφ = 0. At 120°, the waves are partially out of phase, so amplitude must be less than 2A.

The formula: Ares = 2A cos(Δφ/2) = 2A cos(60°) = 2A × ½ = A. Always apply this — don't guess from the phase angle alone.
