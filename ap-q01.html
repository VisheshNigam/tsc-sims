Q1 Kinematics — Velocity & Acceleration
A ball is thrown straight up. At the highest point of its flight, which statement is correct?  (g = 9.8 m/s²)
✓ Correct Answer
B — v = 0, a = 9.8 m/s² downward
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "everything stops at the top" trap: students see "highest point" and assume the ball is "at rest" in every sense — so they choose A (a = 0). But velocity and acceleration are independent quantities. The ball's velocity is momentarily zero, yes — but gravity never switches off.

Why a points down, not up: acceleration always points in the direction of the net force. The only force on the ball in flight is gravity, which always points downward. That's exactly why the ball comes back down — if a were zero at the top, the ball would float there forever.
