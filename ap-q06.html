Q6 Torque — Rotational Equilibrium
A 30 kg child sits 2 m to the left of the pivot of a seesaw. A 20 kg child sits to the right of the pivot. How far from the pivot must the second child sit for the seesaw to balance?
✓ Correct Answer
A — d = (30·2)/20 = 3 m
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "same distance" trap (option B): students assume both children sit the same distance from the pivot. But a lighter child needs a longer lever arm to produce the same torque as a heavier child closer in.

The "inverted ratio" trap (option C): some students apply the mass ratio the wrong way. If you wrote (20/30)·2 = 1.33 m, you've placed the lighter child closer to the pivot — that makes the imbalance worse, not better.

Why d = 3 m: the lighter child needs to sit farther from the pivot to compensate for being lighter. Mass ratio is 30:20 = 3:2, so the distance ratio must invert to 2:3 — the 30 kg child at 2 m, the 20 kg child at 3 m.
