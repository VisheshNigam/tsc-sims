Q2 Dynamics — Newton's Third Law
A heavy truck collides head-on with a small car. During the collision, how do the forces on the two vehicles compare?
✓ Correct Answer
C — equal in magnitude, opposite in direction
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "bigger object pushes harder" trap: students see "heavy truck vs small car" and assume the truck must exert more force. But that confuses force with damage. Newton's third law says the force pair is always equal — Ftruck on car = −Fcar on truck, no exceptions.

Why the car gets wrecked: it's not because the force on the car is bigger. It's because a = F/m, and the car has a tiny m. Same force on the truck barely budges it. Equal forces, dramatically unequal accelerations.
