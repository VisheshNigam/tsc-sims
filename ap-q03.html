Q3 Circular Motion — Net Force Direction
A car drives around a flat circular track at constant speed. In which direction does the net force on the car point?
✓ Correct Answer
B — toward the center of the circle (centripetal)
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "centrifugal force" trap (option C): inside the car, you feel pushed outward — so it seems the force must point outward. But that's an illusion of your own inertia. Your body wants to keep going in a straight line; it's the door that pushes you inward, forcing you to follow the curve.

The "constant speed = no force" trap (option D): constant speed doesn't mean constant velocity. Velocity is a vector — its direction is changing every instant. Changing velocity means there is acceleration, and Newton's second law says there must be a net force to cause it. That net force points toward the center.
