Q5 Momentum — Inelastic Collision
A 3 kg cart moving at 4 m/s collides with a 1 kg cart at rest. The two carts stick together after the collision. What is their final speed?
✓ Correct Answer
B — v_f = (3·4)/(3+1) = 3 m/s
💡 Don't Fall For This
The "average the velocities" trap (option C): students compute (4 + 0)/2 = 2 m/s. That works only when the masses are equal — here the 3 kg cart is three times heavier, so it pulls the centre-of-mass speed closer to 3 m/s, not the midpoint.

The "speed doesn't change" trap (option A): some students assume the moving cart just keeps moving at 4 m/s. But it now has to drag along an extra 1 kg of mass — same momentum, larger total mass, so smaller speed.

Why kinetic energy drops: momentum is conserved, but KE is not. p_initial = 12, p_final = 12 ✓. KE_initial = ½·3·4² = 24 J. KE_final = ½·4·3² = 18 J. About 25% of the kinetic energy is lost to deformation, sound, and heat — that's what makes it inelastic.

Answer to unlock
Total momentum (p = mv)
12.0 kg·m/s — conserved
Total kinetic energy (½mv²)
24.0 J — initial
Before collision
p = 12,  KE = 24 J
Only the 3 kg cart is moving (4 m/s). The 1 kg cart contributes nothing.
After collision (stuck)
p = 12,  KE = 18 J
v_f = 3 m/s. Momentum unchanged; ~25% of KE lost to heat / sound.
(m₁ + m₂)·v_f = m₁·v₁  ⟹  v_f = (3·4)/(3+1) = 12/4 = 3 m/s
Watch the bars: total momentum stays at 100% (conserved), but kinetic energy drops to 75% (lost). That energy gap is the "inelastic" part — converted to deformation, heat, and sound.
