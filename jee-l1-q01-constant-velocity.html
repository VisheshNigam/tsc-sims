Q1 Kinematics — Velocity & Acceleration L1 · Conceptual
An object moving in a straight line has a constant positive velocity. What is true about its acceleration?
✓ Correct Answer
B — The acceleration is zero
💡 Don't Fall For This
Confusing motion with acceleration: If an object is moving fast, it must have acceleration — Wrong. Acceleration only occurs when velocity is changing.

The sign-matching trap: Reading "positive velocity" and instinctively selecting "positive acceleration" is one of the most common errors. The sign of velocity indicates direction; the sign of acceleration indicates the direction of change in velocity — these are completely independent.
